    Length and Text#

    2 pages at most. Be concise, organized, free of errors. Tailor to particular institution.

    Never provide a generic, lengthy biography book to your application. Professors of the admission council have no such leisure hours studying your detailed story. They, or their assistants, will quickly glance through hundreds of application materials in a couple of days. Therefore, you will have to catch their sights by using some accurate remarkable keywords in your PS. Do not make any grammatic or spelling errors which will only downgrade assessment score of your application.

    Main Idea#

    Express your interests (match) and ambitions (passion and motivation) to the program. Demonstrate possible contributions you will make.

    1. Interests. Why you apply, how are you match. Converge with current research.
    2. Abilities, experience. How can you complete.
    3. Ambitions. How will you contribute.

    Show your value and convince the admission board. Applying for college programs is a bilateral selection process. You hunt for a potential degree or certificate, the universities seek for a benefitial client and able researcher. That is the reason you are been assessed.

    Important#

    1. Evidence to abilities to support future study/research.
    2. Alignment to professors and programs.
    3. Familiarities with university’s current research.
    4. Knowledge background.

    As mentioned, you will need to demonstrate your qualifications to meet the requirements. Personal stories do little help to this. Point is that to notify the university about eligibilities of yours.

    Primary Topics#

    1. Interests and related talents, abilities.
    2. Experience to support study, breifly detail it. Articulate knowledge background and its stimulation.
    3. Future goals. Ph.D, subdisciplines interested.
    4. Research interests, agenda. Identify one or two in the subfield.
    5. How are you match? Align research interests with professors and programs.

    Secondary Topics#

    To support future study, completing the program.

    1. Example of attributes, qualities.
    2. Ability to develop ideas, work independency.

    Avoid#

    DO NOT narrate your accomplishments.

    Unless you are a mature successful candidate, never attempt to empathize the admission board with your shallow life/study/internship experiences.
