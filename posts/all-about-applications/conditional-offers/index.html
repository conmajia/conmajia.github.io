    • Conditional Offers from Universities

    A conditional offer is kind of an invitation made by the university to you. As the name suggests, it is only final if you meet certain conditions listed in it.
    Jan. 25, 2021 · 2 min · Greg Conmajia

    Offers are reception passports authorized by universities. They are tickets to future success. Conditional offers, as the name suggests, are made to those partially qualified candidates who lack some of the requirements at the time of application. If you are made a conditional offer, this means you’ve got the potential acceptance from professors but subject to finish tasks listed in the offer.

    Congrats to you! You are at the front gate of your goal. All you have to do is to meet unachieved requirements, which are often language qualifications, recommendation letters, etc., relatively simple and generic.

    Note that only European universities and former British contries such as Singapore and Australia offers such conditional acceptance, while northern America universities, especially those in the United States do not.
