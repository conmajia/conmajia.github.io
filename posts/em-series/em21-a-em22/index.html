    • 4-1 Audio MUX and Stereo Speaker

    Designed to switch audio sources amongst my three PCs and a Mac.
    Jan. 9, 2021 · 3 min · Greg Conmajia

    4-1 audio multiplexer initiated on May 12, 2020, codename EM22.
    Stereo speaker initiated on , codename EM21-A.

    These two devices were made to enjoy audio contents from multiple analog sound sources. EM22 selects output channels and EM21-A amplifies the selection and outputs them via two built-in loudspeakers, or directly via a 3.5mm panel headphone jack.

    Switching between audio channels requires only a simple press on either of the four green buttons on EM22, fairly easy. A built-in buzzer beeps shortly each time a new channel is ready.

    4-1 Audio Multiplexer#

    Audio signals reside in the frequency band from 30 to 20kHz. Therefore, a CD4056 4-way electronic multiplexer chip is capable of transferring such signals.

    Stereo Speaker#

    The speaker features a dual 3-watt audio power amplifier IC PAM8403. The amplifier is powered by a 2-cell paralleled lithium battery pack via a USB type-C charging circuitry. The device is quite power efficient, it can last for approximately 7 days per charge.

    A front-installed 3.5mm is employed to switch audio output from built-in loudspeakers to external headphones or other audio devices. In this scenario, internal speakers become mute.

    Mute feature is accomplished through the mechanical structures of internal metal contacts in a 5-pin 3.5mm audio socket, though the PAM8403 chip provides such mute function per se.

    Power-line Distortion Consideration#

    When processing audio sources that are powered directly from wall sockets (110V/60Hz or 220V/50Hz), battery-powered EM21-A and EM22 suffer from a so-called power-line distortion at corresponding frequency. This is also known as harmonic distortion of the AC power line, where a sinusoidal noise coupled from power lines into audio circuitry, disturbs fine audio signals. Details on the cause of power line distortion can be found at position A and B.

    One possible solution is to use a notch filter, which attenuates signal tremendously at the target frequency as shown (50Hz). Signal at 50Hz is reduced more than 60dB, meaning it is reduced more than 1000 times. Therefore, disturbing noise is almost eliminated. This interrupts desired input at the target frequency, although no fine audio generate a 50Hz or 60Hz sound.
