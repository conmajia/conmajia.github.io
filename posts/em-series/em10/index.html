    • 1A Lithium Battery Charger

    A usage example of TP4056 battery charger.
    Sep. 16, 2018 · 2 min · Greg Conmajia

    codename EM10

    Lithium battery is one of the most commonly used power sources in amateur projects, especially in handheld devices. Under this scenario, an efficient full-featured yet low-profile battery charger which can be transparently embedded is the ultimate requirement for a part selection consideration.

    TP4056 from Top Power ASIC® is such a candidate. This tiny chip is an all-in-one, full featured, integrated solution supporting up to 1 ampere current lithium battery charger and costs less than $0.5 per piece. TP4056 has a complete built-in battery protecting circuitry including short circuit, over current, over/under voltage, over temperature.

    The chip is delivered in a plastic SOP-8 package in the size of 5×6×2mm, smaller than a green bean. With the small package, amateur engineers are capable of either surface mount it on to a printed circuit board (PCB) or hand-wire the chip to build their prototypes.

    Back to TP4056. Figure 2 shows a full featured application of the chip with two LED indicators for charging and standby status and an NTC sensor circuitry for battery temperature detection.

    What is important is the $R_\text{prog}$ resistor which programs the maximum charging current of TP4056. Therefore, a minimal configuration can be established with only $R_\text{prog}$ and the TP4056 per se. Thermal sensoring feature is disabled while the internal 140°C threshold keeps functioning. All decoupling capacitors can be removed in certain scenarios where there is a large tolerance as those in figure 3.

    Finally, with a custom PCB, we are able to build an easy-to-use battery charger module with onboard charging status indicators and thermal sensor in a 0.1" pitch TO-220 package size (15×10×3mm).
